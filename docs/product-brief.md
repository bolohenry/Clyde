# Clyde — Product Brief

## Positioning

Clyde is a conversational product that helps people learn generative AI by applying it to their real life — not through lectures, courses, or abstract prompts, but by starting with what the user actually has going on today.

## Target User

Millennials and Gen X adults (roughly 30–55) who:
- Are comfortable with technology (smartphones, apps, online banking, etc.)
- Have heard about ChatGPT / genAI but haven't adopted it meaningfully
- Don't know *what* to use it for, not *how* to use a computer
- Feel vague anxiety or skepticism ("Is this just hype?")
- Would use AI if someone showed them a concrete win in their own life

## Problem Statement

Most genAI products start with a blank text box and the implicit question: "What do you want?" This is paralyzing for new users who don't yet have a mental model of what AI can do. Courses and tutorials teach concepts but don't connect to the user's real tasks. The result: millions of capable adults try ChatGPT once, don't know what to ask, and never come back.

## Core Insight

People don't need to learn AI in the abstract. They need to *experience* AI being useful on something they already care about — and then understand what just happened so they can do it again.

## Product Shape (Progressive Disclosure)

1. **Conversation first** — Clyde starts by asking about the user's real day in plain language.
2. **Guided flow next** — After a short back-and-forth, Clyde suggests a concrete action (make a plan, draft a message, compare options).
3. **Editable workflow later** — The user can refine, tweak, or branch the help they received.
4. **Freeform workspace after that** — Once confident, the user can start from blank or compose their own workflows.

## Key UX Rules

- The landing page *is* the product experience — no sign-up gate, no onboarding carousel.
- Clyde speaks first with a warm, simple welcome message.
- The first prompt is broadly applicable: "Tell me what you have going on today."
- Clyde mirrors the user's tone, vocabulary, and energy level.
- Transitions from chat to structured help feel natural, not modal.
- Teaching happens by showing, not lecturing.

## What Clyde Is Not

- A course app or tutorial
- A generic blank chat interface
- A work-only productivity tool
- A toy demo or tech showcase

## Success Metrics (Future)

- % of users who send at least one message
- % of users who complete a structured action (plan, list, draft)
- % of users who click "learn how this worked"
- Return visits within 7 days
- Self-reported confidence with AI (pre/post)

## Assumptions for MVP

- Web app, desktop-first but mobile-responsive
- Frontend-only MVP with mocked/local conversation logic
- No authentication required for first-run experience
- AI responses simulated via a lightweight conversation engine (can be swapped for real LLM API later)
- Focus is on UX quality and believable product behavior, not production AI
